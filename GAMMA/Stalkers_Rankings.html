🔬 Data Pre-Processing

The numbers on this page aren't raw game logs from the mod, they've already been cleaned before import.

1. Minimum sample size. Each unique combination of faction + armor + weapon + ammo must have enough kills recorded; if the threshold is not met it's not making it to the rankings.

2. Big outliers are removed from the dataset (IQR on raw TTK). Helps remove "freak events" that would poison the data, especially on low sample sizes.

TTK - recorded in-game
"What happened"
Time from first shot on target to completed kill measured during gameplay. The values are passed through an outlier filter (IQR fence). Melting pot of body parts hit, accuracy, fire rate, movement, enemy behaviour and skill. Very "noisy", but a real in-game metric.
STK - standardized TTK
"How fast is the weapon itself?"
Theoretical minimum kill time if every bullet hit: (HTK − 1) / (RPM / 60). See it as the "weapon's ceiling". Mostly weapon mechanics to dampen skill and RNG noise. HtK are still based on damage dealt and body parts hit, so less noisy but still linked to in-game perf.
TTTK - true TTK
"Accounting for real accuracy"
Kinda bridges STK and TTK: (HTK / Acc − 1) / (RPM / 60). Takes your accuracy and asks how long it realistically takes to land enough hits to kill. A fast gun with poor accuracy can score worse than a slower gun with good aim. Balance of skill and weapon performance.
Master TTK - ranking metric
"Best of both worlds"
Weighted blend across all TTKs formulas. TTK has the most weight as it's the only "true metric", cleaned of outliers. TTTK is a good balance of all recorded data. STK is an anchor to what the weapon could achieve in the best hands.

⚡ Best
"lowest Master TTK"
Ranks by weighted average Master TTK across all armor tiers. Lower is better. The Low Data badge appears when any active tier has fewer than 20 kills.
💸 Cheapest
"lowest cost/kill"
Average roubles spent per kill. Cheap and fast will be ranked at the top. Secondary column shows Master TTK so you can see what you're trading in speed for money.
⚖️ Balanced
"real efficiency"
Normalises each weapon's Master TTK and Cost as a weighted score: TTK × 60% + Cost × 40%. TTK has higher weight as kill speed is the primary combat variable. Ties (within 2%) are grouped.
Reasoning behind all this: The gap between STK and TTTK is your accuracy penalty (how much time missed shots add). A small gap means you're using the weapon efficiently; a large gap means poor accuracy (or shooting beyond effective range etc.. both skill issues, but in a different way). The gap between TTTK and TTK is real-world combat noise: movement, cover peeking, trigger discipline. Because TTK is outlier-filtered from the raw logs, a regular high TTK relative to TTTK means that a weapon is truly underperforming, not just skill issues.
